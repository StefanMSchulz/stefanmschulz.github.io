Hi, I'm Stefan.

Currently, I am pursuing my Master's in Computer Science at the University of Bonn, Germany. I am also a student research assistant for PD Dr. Volker Steinhage working on image inpainting techniques to infer missing surface temperature data due to cloud occlusion. My main contributions to this project are literature review, coming up with possible ideas, and implementation of a novel approach in 3D to incorporate temporal information as well. Additionally, I am a research student assistant for Prof. Dr. Thomas Schultz where I am working on instance aware segmentation for drusen in OCT-Scans as well as identifying potential drusen types based on their characteristics.

Throughout my bachelor studies I have focused on Machine Learning and Robotics. Specifically Computer Vision has been a huge part of my time as an undergrad. Mainly, I am interested in applying Machine Learning to provide good to the community. This is why I decided to write my bachelor thesis on "Predicting AMD Stage and Visual Function from Drusen Ultrastruture". Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) is a disease that has no cure yet but affect many millions of people all around the globe. To me this is a major factor that I considered when doing my thesis, it was meant to be something meaningful. I am striving to get better, learn more and hopefully, one day, I will be able to help find a cure to a disease. Therefore, specializing even further in the medical domain of Machine Learning and Computer Vision was a no-brainer and I am happy to be able to do what I want.
